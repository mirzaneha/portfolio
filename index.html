Artifact 01 — Workshop 1 — Assignment 1.4

From Questions to Insights — Building a Data Insights Assistant

AI Lab

A hands-on AI lab documenting the end-to-end experience of exploring LLMs, Custom GPTs, and research AI tools — culminating in the design and build of a working AI chatbot, the Data Insights Assistant, built on Chatbase.co. For a data analytics audience, this artifact answers: can this person use AI tools to solve a real problem that data professionals face every day?

Objective

To gain direct hands-on experience with multiple AI tools and apply a Design Thinking framework to build a practical, purpose-built AI assistant that helps data analysts and business users identify the right questions to ask their data before jumping into analysis.

Description

The Data Insights Assistant is a chatbot that helps users slow down before they start analyzing. Rather than generating charts or running queries, it guides users through defining their business problem, identifying the right analytical approach, and asking better questions of their data — the step most analysts skip.

Process

1

LLM Practice (Google Gemini): Ran a prompt about AI transforming data analytics across 3 separate Gemini conversations. Chat 1 focused on automation, Chat 2 on strategic role shift, Chat 3 on specific tools like Tableau AI and Power BI Copilot. Key lesson: run multiple chats and combine the best elements from each rather than relying on one response.

2

Custom GPT (Data Analyst GPT): Used Data Analyst GPT to explore a sales dataset analysis approach. It asked clarifying business context questions before making suggestions — producing much more targeted output than a generic LLM. The final data analysis plan it produced was something I could use in a real project immediately.

3

STORM AI: Researched AI in Business Intelligence and Data Analytics. STORM approached the topic from business leader, data professional, technology vendor, and end user perspectives simultaneously — producing a balanced article that included natural language querying and the evolving analyst role.

4

Design Thinking: Empathy — analyst who has data but does not know what questions to ask. Define — people jump into tools before defining the business problem. Ideate — questions-first advisor beats SQL generator. Prototype — Data Insights Assistant on Chatbase.co. Test — three different analysis scenarios.

5

Build & Iterate: Iteration 1 jumped to analysis techniques without understanding business context. Iteration 2 added business problem question first — quality improved immediately. Iteration 3 added "go deeper" follow-up step — conversations felt like genuine consulting sessions.

Value Proposition

For data analytics teams and business leaders, this artifact demonstrates something more valuable than technical credentials — the ability to identify a real user frustration, design a human-centered solution, and iterate toward something genuinely useful. The Data Insights Assistant solves a problem every data analyst knows: people jump to answers before they understand the question. Building a tool that fixes that problem shows I understand what good analysis actually requires.

Unique Value

My data analytics background shaped this bot in a way a student without that experience would not have approached it. I knew from firsthand experience that the biggest bottleneck in analytics is not the tools or the data — it is the quality of the questions being asked. That insight drove every design decision in the Data Insights Assistant, from the opening message to the five-step conversation flow.

Relevance

As AI tools flood into analytics workflows, the professionals who will create the most value are not those who automate the fastest but those who ask the best questions before automating anything. This artifact demonstrates exactly that mindset — and the process of building it gave me practical experience with the AI tools that are already transforming the data analytics profession I work in every day.
